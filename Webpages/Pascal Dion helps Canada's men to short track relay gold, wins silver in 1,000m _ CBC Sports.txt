Social Sharing Canadian women, mixed squad also capture relay silver in Debrecen, Hungary CBC Sports · Posted: Nov 21, 2021 10:59 AM ET | Last Updated: 2 hours ago Montreal's Pascal Dion made it 3-for-3 in podium finishes this season over 1,000 metres, placing second to South Korea's Hwang Daeheon at a World Cup event on Sunday in Debrecen, Hungary. He also helped Canada's men to relay gold in the 5,000 event. (Mladen Antonov/AFP via Getty Images/File) comments Pascal Dion was back to his medal-winning ways in the 1,000-metre distance on Sunday and also contributed to a gold performance in men's relay before leaving Hungary. The Montreal speed skater joined Jordan-Pierre Gilles, Charles Hamelin and Steve Dubois atop the medal podium in the 5,000 event after posting a time of six minutes 44.045 seconds to beat South Korea (6:44.892) and the host Hungarians (6:45.003). WATCH | Canada's men top medal podium in 5,000-metre relay: Canada captures gold in Men's 5,000m short track relay 3 hours ago 13:30The Canadian men's team placed first in the 5,000-metre short track relay event during the ISU World Cup Short Track competition in Debrecen, Hungary. 13:30 Earlier, Dion stopped the clock in 1:25.698 for silver in a short track World Cup race in Debrecen. The 27-year-old has reached the medal podium in each of his 1,000 races this season. He won bronze on Oct. 31 in Nagoya, Japan, one week after placing third in Beijing. Hwang Daeheon of South Korea was victorious in Sunday's competition in 1:25.425, with Dutch skater Itzhak de Laat rounding out the podium in 1:26.211. Pascal Dion silver tops trio of Canadian short track medallists Canada's Dion skates to World Cup short track bronze in Beijing Dion picked up his first World Cup medal in the men's 1,500 on Saturday, taking silver behind China's Ren Ziwei. Suzanne Schulting of the Netherlands posted a winning time of 1:31.475, followed by South Korea's Choi Minjeon (1:31.789) and Poland's Natalia Maliszewska (1:31.950). Canada's Sarault captures short track silver at Beijing World Cup event Sarault later contributed to a silver medal performance in the women's relay. The 21-year-old teamed with Alyson Charles, Florence Brunelle and Kim Boutin to post a time of 4:06.789 over 3,000 metres. The Netherlands won in 4:06.769 and China was third in 4:06.782. Camille de Serres-Rainville, Charles Hamelin, Steve Dubois and Brunelle placed second over 2,000 metres in 2:39.651 in mixed relay. China prevailed in 2:39.386 and France was third in 2:40.417.
